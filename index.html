THE INNER PLANETS IN OUR SOLAR SYSTEM

Mercury

The smallest planet and the closest to the Sun. It has no atmosphere and experiences extreme temperature swings.

Venus

Known for its thick, toxic atmosphere of carbon dioxide, which traps heat and makes it the hottest planet in the solar system. It is similar in size to Earth.

Earth

Our home planet, and the only one known to support life. It has an atmosphere rich in nitrogen and oxygen, and its surface is dominated by water and land.

Mars

Often called the "Red Planet" due to the iron oxide in its soil. It has a thin atmosphere and features like volcanoes and dry riverbeds, suggesting that water may have once flowed on its surface.
